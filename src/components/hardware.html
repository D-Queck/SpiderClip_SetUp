Hardware-Sensor-Platform

The hardware sensor platform acts as the periphery of the communication interface. It consists of a 3D-printed housing and a sensor platform with a communication module.

3D-Housing

The SpiderClip enclosure is designed as a three-piece shell that neatly houses all electronics – sensor board, battery and communication module. Each shell half plus a top-cap are printed separately on any standard FDM or SLA printer. To fasten the parts you can either use a drop of strong adhesive or insert M2.5 screws into the integrated bores and alignment pins.

  • Vive Tracker 1.0: Two Ø3 mm bores line up with the tracker’s mounting lugs. Snap the tracker in and secure with screws.
  • Vive Tracker 2.0 / 3.0: Uses a slightly adjusted hole pattern and reinforced clip geometry – alignment pegs guarantee a flush fit.
  • Controller-Loop Mount: Designed for Quest 2/3 or Index controllers. Feed a nylon strap through side slots and tighten around the grip.

In the 3D visualization aboth all drill-holes, slots and alignment pins are highlighted in red, so you can instantly see where to glue or where to drive screws for each variant.

Sensor Platform

At the heart of the Hardware Sensor Platform sits an Arduino Nano 33 IoT, powered by a single 3.7 V LiPo cell, which communicates via I²C (SCL, SDA) with a SparkFun Bio Sensor Hub (pulse oximeter & heart‐rate). The Nano acts as I²C‐master: it polls the Bio Sensor for data and relays measurements over USB or BLE to the host system.

You can power the Nano 33 IoT either via its Micro-USB port (e.g. from your PC or a USB supply) or directly from the LiPo battery. Both the sensor hub and the Nano share the same 3.7 V rail.

Before uploading the firmware, install the following Arduino libraries:

  • <ArduinoBLE.h>
  • <Arduino_LSM6DS3.h>
  • <SparkFun_Bio_Sensor_Hub_Library.h>
  • <Wire.h> (in der Regel bereits im Kern-Package enthalten)
